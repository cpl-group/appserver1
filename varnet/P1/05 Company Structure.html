History

Consultant for Efficient Buildings (CEB) was founded in 1984 and began providing electrical consulting services to the New York City Real Estate industry in early 1985.  As an electrical consulting firm, CEB did not provide design engineering services.  Rather, CEB focused its attention on electrical measurements, thermal imaging, field testing and the ‘mapping’ electrical systems, services that to this day are typically not performed by design engineers. 

 

By 1988, CEB was recognized in the industry for its introduction of the Power Grid Identification, or PGI, (Exhibit E), a labor intensive service aimed at identifying and detailing primary building electrical distribution systems via one line electrical schematics.  The PGI was computer generated, color coded, easy to follow, inclusive of sub metering point, and complete with power availability charts calculating designed watt per square foot availability from ‘bottleneck’ capacity over square footage served.  The PGI was an instant Hit!

 

In addition to the PGI, CEB provided thermal imaging services, power monitoring and availability services, and general electrical consulting.  CEB’s work force consisted primarily of skilled and versatile technicians trained in circuit tracing, surveys and research.

 

By 1989, CEB had expanded its core services into electronic sub meters and metering systems, a market that boomed during the 1980’s.  By 1992, CEB sold, installed, and maintained electronic meters and metering system.  By 1993, CEB has already positioned itself well in the electronic sub meter industry and in 1994, as deregulation continued to unfold, CEB saw an opportunity in data gathering and data profiling.  In 1995, CEB founded Advanced Metering Systems (AMS). 

 

Advanced Metering System focused on reading meters, billing energy usage on behalf of landlords, rent inclusion surveys, overtime HVAC surveys, public lighting and power surveys, fuel adjustment calculations and other services traditionally performed by metering companies.  Advanced Metering Systems, however, was a latecomer to the New York City metering industry and took advantage of deregulation by designing and implementing proprietary revenue billing software capable of acquiring and processing interval data.  By the fall of 1997, AMS was profiling one hour energy data.

 

By mid 1999, AMS was well established and CEB introduced a full engineering department.  CEB was now rock solid.  But all along these companies operated independently.  It became very clear that the combination of these two entities would be the future of our firm.  By integrating electrical grid diagrams with energy usage data, we would have information covering all aspects of building energy distribution and the monetary recovery of energy usage.  For the next year or so, our management efforts were focused on combining data and creating an energy information system.

 

In January of 2001, General Energy Services (Genergy) emerged from strategic alliance between CEB and AMS.  Along with these assets, Genergy integrate Information Technology (IT) services and a new concept called gEnergyOne.

 

Overview

Genergy is made up of five departments; Engineering Services, Technical Services, Energy Services, IT Services and Accounting.  Although different departments specialize in different tasks, segregation among departments and services is non existent.  In fact, our ability to combine different disciplines and services into one concise web enabled information system is the root of our success.  Genergy custom tailors and combines thermal imaging services, riser diagrams, power availability, engineering, electric rent inclusion surveys and management, energy usage information, energy costs, monetary recovery information, and metering on a secured web enabled, real time environment.  Our management team is entrepreneurial, has a vast in depth knowledge of Real Estate management and focused on business operation.

Genergy has taken conventional ‘stand alone’ services like the PGI, the IRI or the sub meter revenue billing, which are at one point or other performed by virtually every class A ownership or management, and has transformed these services into a fully integrated and relational energy information system called gEnergyOne.  The objective is to sign long term contracts and populate our energy information system with various data from a multitude of clients.  This data would not only serve our clients in their immediate needs, but would provide them with the baseline information for wise management and energy choices.  Genergy’s vision is to acquire four thousand new sites within the next five years, bringing our staff from less than thirty to over one thousand people.  Refer to Exhibit G for growth details.

 

The Departments

Engineering Services (EG) – Responsible for all engineering survey and design services, property condition surveys, specialty surveys, PGI and IRI quality assurance, code compliance studies, and general engineering consulting.

 

Technical Services (TE) – Responsible for all PGI and IRI surveys, power availability surveys, drawing reviews and resulting PGI updates, lease review, power quality installations and reporting, specialty product research, equipment and tool tracking, cross training, metering surveys and metering system design, metering sales and service, meter start up reports, meter calibration, CAD design, and general electrical consulting.

 

Energy Services (ES) – Responsible for meter reading schedules, sub meter billing services, account maintenance, management reports, ERI surveys and reports, PLP surveys, HVAC surveys, load audits, utility bill audits, utility rate information, lease interpretation and general energy consulting.

 

Information Technology (IT) – Responsible for maintenance of all computing equipment, peripherals, manuals and registrations, software and hardware development and implementation, network maintenance and security, back up procedures, internal office automation, new technology research, web hosting, maintenance of customer login and password, office phone and data lines, and E Mail/Internet monitoring.

 

Accounting (AC) – Responsible for matters concerning payroll, payroll taxes, benefits, employee status, tracking of vacation and personal time, client status, vendor status, time sheets and job cost.
