Company Details        

 

Employment Policies

 

1.  Equal Employment Opportunity

 

General Energy Services, Inc.  (“Genergy” or the “Company”) is an equal opportunity employer that does not discriminate on the basis of race, religion, color, sex, national origin, age, citizenship, ancestry, marital status, sexual orientation, pregnancy, childbirth and related medical conditions, veteran status, disability or any other characteristic prohibited by federal, state or local law.  The Company is dedicated to insuring the fulfillment of this policy with respect to hiring, placement, selection for training, promotion, transfer, demotion, layoffs, termination, recruitment, advertising, rates of pay or other forms of compensation and general treatment during employment.

 

The Company will endeavor to make a reasonable accommodation to the known physical or mental limitations of a qualified applicant or employee with a disability unless the accommodation would impose an undue hardship on the operation of our business.

 

The Company prohibits sexual harassment of any employee by another employee or supervisor.  The Company’s policy and procedure concerning complaints of sexual harassment is fully described in this handbook. 

 

Any employee with any concerns or complaints of any type of discrimination in the workplace is encouraged to bring those issues to the attention of his or her supervisor, to Davide Gristina or to any member of management with whom the employee feels comfortable.  Employees may make reports or raise concerns regarding discrimination without fear of reprisal.  All reports will be treated discreetly and will be promptly and thoroughly investigated.

 

Any employee of the Company who engages in unlawful discrimination will be subject to discipline, which may include termination.

 

2.  Employment at Will

 

It is the policy of the Company that employees who do not have a written employment contract with the Company for a specific fixed term of employment are employed at the will of the Company for an indefinite period and are subject to termination at any time, for any reason, with or without cause or notice.  At the same time, such employees may terminate their employment at any time for any reason.  No Company representative is authorized to modify this policy for any employee or to enter into any agreement, oral or written, contrary to this policy.

 

3.  Eligibility for Employment

           

In compliance with federal law, the Company will only hire individuals who are either authorized to work in the U.S. or are U.S. citizens.  Offers of employment are conditional on the applicant’s presentation of proper documentation of evidence of identity and authorization to work in the U.S. no later than 3 business days after commencement of employment.

 

4.  Initial Employment Period

 

The purpose of the initial employment period is to give the Company a reasonable period of time to evaluate the employee’s performance.  The initial employment period is three months. 

 

During this time, the employee will not be provided with any health insurance benefits, nor will the employee be eligible to receive holiday hours, vacation hours, or personal hours.   At the end of the initial employment period, the Company will conduct a performance appraisal.  If the performance appraisal is satisfactory, all benefits will go into effect at this time. 

 

Compensation

 

1.  Payroll

 

All full-time salaried employees and part-time employees are paid weekly on Mondays. 

                       

Employees will be paid by check and will receive an itemized statement each pay period.  If the employee has any questions regarding his/her salary or deductions, please contact Tara Clarke in the Accounting Department.  

 

2.   Direct Deposit

 

The Company provides the option of Direct Deposit. Employee’s pay will be deposited directly into their bank or credit union.  The deposit account can be either a checking or savings account.  The account can be at most any financial institutions in New York.  If you sign up for Direct Deposit, you will continue to receive your regular earnings statement (or check stub).

 

3.   TransitChek Program

 

The TransitChek Program serves as a pre-tax benefit.  Employee’s can set aside up to a maximum of One Hundred dollars ($100.00) once per month from their paycheck for either a TransitChek or a Metrocard to help pay for their commute to work.

 

4.  Performance Reviews

 

The Company will conduct performance reviews of all employees on a semi-annual basis.    Any salary increases will be given on an annual basis.

 

5.  Overtime

 

The Company does not pay overtime to salaried employees without prior approval of upper management.  

                       

6.  Health Insurance Plans

 

The Company’s health insurance plan provides employees and their dependents access to medical and dental insurance benefits.   All full-time employees are eligible to participate in the health insurance plan (“eligible employees”). 

 

All eligible employees may participate in the health insurance plan subject to all terms and conditions of the agreement between the Company and the insurance carrier.

 

Health insurance coverage is effective on the first of the month following successful completion of the initial employment period. 

 

Details of the health insurance plan are described in the Summary Plan Description annexed to this handbook.  Information on cost of coverage will be provided in advance of enrollment to eligible employees.

 

  6.  Life Insurance

 

The Company’s Group Life Insurance Policy provides a $25,000 death benefit per employee once the initial employment period has been completed.  There is a 35% cutback for employees that are of the age 65 and over and a 50% cutback for employees that are of the age 70 or over.

 

 

401K Employment Savings Plan

 

 

1.  Eligibility for Plan Participation

 

 As an employee, you will be eligible to participate in the Plan on the first day of each the first, fourth, seventh and tenth month of the plan year immediately following your attaining age 21 and completing 3 month(s) of service.

 

2.  Type of Plan

                       

The Plan type is commonly known as a 401(k) employee savings plan.

 

3.  Employee Savings Contributions

 

The employee savings portion of the Plan permits you to elect to defer up to 25% of your compensation (subject to special IRS 401(k) nondiscrimination rules), provided such amount does not exceed $10,500 per year or such additional amount for the year 2001 or later years prescribed by the IRS as adjustments for cost in living increases.  You will not be required to pay income tax on the amount of your compensation you elect to defer into the Plan until you receive a distribution from the Plan.

 

4.  Company Matching Contributions

                       

In addition to the amount of compensation you elect to defer into the plan, the company will make matching contributions to the plan on your behalf.  Each year the Company will determine the level of matching contributions, if any, based on each dollar you elect to contribute into the plan.  For the calendar year 2001 the matching % is 2.5.

 

5.  Vesting in Contributions

           

You are always 100% vested (nonforfeitable) in the compensation you elect to defer into the plan.

 

If you terminate employment prior to normal retirement age for any reason other than death or disability, your interest in Company contributions made to the Plan for your benefit becomes vested in accordance with the following schedule:

                                   

Years of Service                                                                       Percent  of

                                                                                    Nonforfeitable Interest

 

Any service less than 2 years                                         0%

At least two (2) years                                                    20%

At least three (3) years                                                  40%

At least four (4) years                                                   60%

At least five (5) years                                                    80%

At least six (6) or more years                                         100%

 

 

In general, you will receive a year of service credit for purposes of the vesting schedule for all Plan Years (even though not consecutive) during which you work at least 1,000 hours for the Company.  You will not receive credit for years of service with the Company prior to the establishment of the Plan.  If you complete 1,000 hours of service during a Plan Year, you will receive vesting credit for that Plan Year even though not employed on the last day of the Plan Year.  If you terminate employment before you interest becomes 100% vested, you will forfeit the non-vested portion of you interest under the Plan’s “break in service” rule.

 

 

6.  Vesting Upon Normal Retirement Age, Death or Disability

 

 Your interest in all contributions made to the Plan for your benefit becomes 100% vested upon your attaining the Plan’s normal retirement age, age 65, or if your terminate employment because of death or disability.

 

7.  Distribution From the Plan    

 

Distributions will be made to you from the Plan following your retirement, death, disability or termination of employment.

 

8.  Participant Loans

 

You may apply to the Trustee for a loan form the Plan.  Your application must be in writing on forms to be provided to you by the Committee.  The Committee will grant loan on a nondiscriminatory basis.

 

 

Attendance and Time Off

 

1.  Attendance Policy

 

All employees are expected to report to work at the scheduled time and place.  The Company’s regular hours of operation are from 6:00 a.m. to 6:00 p.m.    All employees are required to work eight and one half (8 1/2) hours per day, which includes a one half hour (1/2) lunch break.   In the event an employee is going to be late, he/she should contact his/her Supervisor immediately.   In the event of repeated tardiness, the Company reserves the right to reduce an employee’s pay check in proportion to the extent of the tardiness and such employees are also subject to discipline, which may include termination. 

 

2.  Holidays

 

Full-time salaried employees of the Company will receive ten paid holidays:  New Years Day, Martin Luther King Day, Washington’s Birthday, Memorial Day, Independence Day, Labor Day, Columbus Day, Thanksgiving Day (and the following day) and Christmas Day.

 

The Company, at its discretion, may allow additional paid days preceding or following the holiday, as it deems appropriate.             

 

3.  Personal Hours

 

Full-time salaried employees employed at least three months are granted personal hours for reasons of sickness, religious observance, family obligations or for any reason of a private or personal nature.  An employee will earn eight (8) personal hours for each two (2) months of service.  No more than eight (8) personal hours may be taken consecutively.   All personal hours are subject to the approval of the Company.  The Company reserves the right to assign personal hours to employees on days when the Company’s regular hours of operation are suspended or the Company is closed.  Personal hours may not be taken the day before or the day after a scheduled holiday.  Personal hours are to be used during the calendar year and may not be carried over to the next year.  The employee will be paid for unused personal hours at the end of the calendar year.   Upon the employee’s termination or resignation, personal hours will be paid.

 

4.  Vacation Policy

 

Full-time employees will be entitled to paid vacation based upon the following schedule:

                                    Years of Employment                Paid Vacation Hours

 

                                                 1                                             40

                                                 2                                             80

                                                 4                                             120

                                                10                                            160

                                                20                                             240

                                               

Full-time employees with less than one year of service will not be entitled to any paid vacation hours.

 

Full-time employees cannot take more 80 hours of vacation time at one time.

 

Each employee must submit his/her proposed vacation schedule 45 days in advance of any requested vacation time of forty hours (40) hours or more and one (1) week in advance of any requested vacation time of thirty two (32) hours or less. All requests for vacation must be approved by the employee’s immediate supervisor and upper management.   Vacation may be taken before or after a holiday. 

 

All allotted vacation hours must be taken in the calendar year in which they are earned.  No vacation hours may be carried over into the following calendar year.   Remaining earned but unused vacation hours will be paid at the end of the calendar year. 

 

5.  Jury Duty

 

The Company realizes that it is the obligation of all U.S. Citizens to serve on a jury when summoned to do so. 

 

Employees will be paid $40 per day for the first three days of service, in accordance with state law.   No additional compensation will be paid after the first three days of service.  

 

 

Leaves of Absence

 

1.  Bereavement Leave

 

In the event of a death in an employee’s immediate family, a leave of absence of 16 hours with pay will be granted.  For purposes of this policy, “immediate family” is defined to include an employee’s spouse, child, parent, brother, brother-in-law, sister-in-law, grandparent, grandchild, mother-in-law, father-in-law, first cousin, first niece, first nephew or other resident of the household.  Personal hours may be taken in addition to this time.  The employee should notify his/her immediate supervisor or any member of upper management of a death in the family.  

 

Employees may request time off to attend funerals for other than immediate family members, but will not receive pay for the time off unless personal or vacation hours are used to cover the absence.

 

2.  Maternity Leave

 

Employees may take maternity leave pursuant to the same criteria that govern short-term disability leave.

 

3.  Short Term Disability Leave

 

The Company provides an insurance program for employees who become disabled because of injuries or sicknesses which have no connection to their employment in accordance with New York State’s disability benefits law. 

 

Disability Benefits are paid for off-the-job injury, sickness, and for disabilities arising from a pregnancy to eligible employees.   An employee’s eligibility is determined in accordance with applicable law.  To be eligible, an employee must have worked for the Company for at least four consecutive weeks immediately preceding the disability.   The employee is responsible for filing a Form DB-450 (obtained from the Company) within 30 days after the disability begins.  Failure to file this form within the prescribed time limit may result in penalties, disallowance of claim, or both.

 

If it is determined that the employee is eligible for benefits, the first payment of benefits is due on the 14th day of the disability and will continue to be paid every two weeks thereafter provided the disability continues.  Payments may not exceed 26 weeks for any one period of disability, nor may an employee receive more than 26 weeks of benefits during any 52 consecutive weeks, regardless of the number of periods of disability during this period.  Benefits are never paid for the first week of disability.   Time limits, eligibility requirements, and benefits are determined by applicable law and are subject to change.  Please refer to the policy attached or ask the Company’s representative for more detailed information. 

 

In the event that the employee exceeds time limits on any leave granted pursuant to local, state or federal laws, the employer reserves the right to fill the position in accordance with applicable state, federal and local law. 

 

 

Personnel Policies

 

1.  Sexual Harassment

           

It is the Company’s policy to prohibit sexual harassment of one employee by another employee or supervisor.  Sexual harassment, whether it is suggestive or coercive, is an infringement upon the rights of all employees, a form of sex discrimination and a violation of the Equal Employment Opportunity laws.  While it is not easy to define precisely what harassment is, it certainly includes unwelcome sexual advances, requests of sexual favors and other verbal or physical conduct of a sexual nature such as uninvited touching or sexually related comments or when:

 

*submission to such conduct is made either explicitly or implicitly a term or condition of an individual’s employment,

 

*submission to or rejection of such conduct by an individual is used as the basis for employment decisions affecting such individual, or

 

*such conduct has the purpose or effect of interfering with an individual’s work performance or creating an intimidating, hostile or offensive working environment

 

It is important to note that sexual harassment can be directed at men as well as women and that co-workers as well as management or supervisory personnel may be guilty of sexual harassment.

 

All members of management will be held accountable for the effective administration of this policy.

 

Any employee who feels that they have been subjected to or is aware of possible sexual harassment should immediately report the matter to his/her immediate supervisor, any member of management with whom he/she feels comfortable or Danilo Gristina.  Every report of actual or perceived sexual harassment will be investigated and corrective action taken where appropriate.  No retaliation will be taken against anyone for making a report under this policy.

 

Violations of this policy will not be permitted and will result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.

 

2.  Confidential and Proprietary Information

 

Confidential Information. 

Employees shall not, either during the term of their employment or after the termination of their employment for any reason, divulge, furnish or make accessible to any person, firm, corporation or

 

other business entity, the Company’s customer list or any information, technical data, research, business plans or strategies, computer programs, data bases, know-how, or trade secrets relating to the business, business practices, marketing strategies, market intelligence, data, analyses, methods, processes, business plans, partnering, ventures, services, techniques, projects, equipment, customers, vendors, business contacts, prices, financing, contracts with third parties or other confidential non-public aspects of the business of the Company, and/or the Company’s affiliates, except as required in good faith during the term of the employee’s employment or by law, without the prior written consent of the Company. 

 

Proprietary Information. 

(a)  All information and know-how related to the activities of the Company, whether or not in writing, of a private, secret or confidential nature, concerning the Company’s business or financial affairs (collectively, “Proprietary Information”) is the exclusive property of the Company.  By way of illustration, but not limitation, Proprietary Information may include inventions, products, processes, methods, techniques, formulas, research, business plans, strategies, compositions, compounds, projects, developments, plans, research data, research or other material developed by the employee or any third party for the Company, clinical data, financial data, market intelligence, partnering, potential ventures, personnel data, computer programs, data bases, compilations, analyses and customer and supplier lists.  Employees are not permitted to disclose any Proprietary Information to others outside the Company or use the same for any unauthorized purposes without written approval by an officer of the Company, either during the term of their employment or after the term of their employment, unless and until such Proprietary Information has become public knowledge without fault by the Employee. 

 

(b)  All files, letters, memoranda, correspondence, reports, analyses, compilations, records, data, sketches, drawings, laboratory notebooks, program listings, or other written, photographic, or other tangible material containing Proprietary Information, whether created by an employee or others, which shall come into an employee’s custody or possession, shall be and are the exclusive property of the Company to be used by employees only in the performance of their duties for the Company.

 

(c)  An employee’s obligation not to disclose or use information, know-how and records of the types set forth in paragraphs (a) and (b) above, also extends to such types of information, know-how, records and tangible property of customers of the Company or suppliers to the Company or other third parties who may have disclosed or entrusted the same to the Company or to the employee in the course of the Company’s business.

 

Developments:

(a)  Employees shall make full and prompt disclosure to the Company of all inventions, improvements, discoveries, methods, developments, market intelligence, research data, analyses, compilations, software, and works of authorship, related to the activities of the Company of any nature whatsoever, whether patentable or not, which are created, made, conceived or reduced to practice by the employee or under the employee’s direction or jointly with others during the term of the employee’s employment, whether or not during normal working hours, or on the premises of the Company (all of which are collectively referred to in this Section as “Developments”).

 

(b)  Employees assign to the Company (or any person or entity designated by the Company) all of their right, title, and interest in and to all Developments and all related patents, patent applications, copyrights and copyright applications.  All work fixed in a tangible medium of expression shall be deemed a work made for hire under the US Copyright Act such that the work is owned by the Company at the moment of creation.

 

(c)  Employees shall cooperate fully with the Company, both during and after the term of their employment, with respect to the procurement, maintenance and enforcement of copyrights and patents (both in the United States and foreign countries) relating to Developments.  Employees are required to sign all papers, including, without limitation, copyright applications, patent applications, declarations, oaths, formal assignments, assignment of priority rights, and powers of attorney, which the Company may deem necessary or desirable in order to protect its rights and interest in any Development.

 

3.  Non-Competition

 

 For a period of 2 years following either the employees’ voluntary termination of employment with the Company or termination for cause by the Company, employees are not permitted to engage in any capacity in any business substantially similar to or in competition with the business conducted by the Company in any geographical area in which the Company (or any of its affiliates) transacts such business, during his/her employment.

 

In this context, termination for cause is defined as (1) the Employee’s willful and continued failure to substantially perform his/her duties after written notice by the Company and ten (10) days within which to cure any such failure;  (2) the Employee’s violation of any Company policy outlined in this Handbook;  (3)  the Employee’s conviction for any felony or any misdemeanor involving moral turpitude;  (4)  the Employee’s willful misconduct in the performance of his/her duties, including any act of dishonesty which has or could have a material impact on the Company;  (5)  fraud, embezzlement or misappropriation by the Employee relating to the Company or any of its funds, properties or assets; (6) the Employee acting in any manner or making any statements not at the direction of the Company which the Company reasonably determines to be detrimental or damaging to the reputation, operations, prospects or business relations of the Company or its affiliates; or (7) the Employee voluntarily terminating employment with the Company to avoid termination for cause by the Company.

 

In this context, a business substantially similar to or in competition with the business of the Company means (i) any business that provides sub-metering services, including but not limited to, meter reading, or meter billing; (ii) any other business engaged in the marketing, sale, installation, distribution and servicing of any type of sub-metering services, meter reading, or meter billing to commercial or residential subscribers; (iii) any business which provides, compiles or analyzes data concerning sub-metering and energy services or energy use to energy service providers, consumers or commercial entities; and (iv) any energy service provider.

 

4.  Non-Solicitation

 

All Company relationships, whether or not contractual, including but not limited to relationships with employees, contractors, consultants, partners (collectively “Relationships”) are the sole property of the Company.  Both during the term of their employment with the Company and after the termination of their employment for any reason by the Company, unless the Company provides written consent, employees are not permitted whether, directly or indirectly, to provide information, including but not limited to employee lists, resumes, independent contractor agreements, employment information and contact information, to other entities or individuals.  Employees may not interfere with these Relationships by soliciting individuals or entities, directly or indirectly, to work with or for any person or entity external to the Company without the written consent of the Company, either during or for a period of one (1) year after the termination of the employee’s employment.   All Company relationships with customers, partners, resellers, vendors and suppliers and all information, whether or not in writing, is and shall be the exclusive property of the Company (collectively, “Customer Information”).  Customer Information shall not be used outside the duties of the employee’s position with the Company without the written consent of the Company, either during or for a period of one (1) year after the termination of the employee’s employment.

 

5.  Dress Policy

 

Employees may dress is business casual attire, but will be expected to dress in formal business wear when meeting with clients, either inside or outside the Company’s offices.  

 

6.  Smoking Policy

 

In order to comply with state and local laws, the Company has prohibited smoking in the following areas:

 

All enclosed areas within “public places”, including the reception area, hallways, restrooms,      waiting areas, and elevators;

 

All common work areas;

           

Conference rooms, including guest rooms and meeting rooms unless every person in the room agrees to allow smoking;

 

Smoking is permitted in designated smoking areas and single private offices when the door is closed.

 

Employees are protected from retaliatory action or from being subjected to

any adverse personnel action for exercising or attempting to exercise his/her right under the smoking policy.

 

7.  Workers’ Compensation

 

The Company provides a workers’ compensation insurance program for employees in accordance with Workers’ Compensation Law. 

 

Employees who sustain work-related injuries or illness should inform their supervisor immediately.  No matter how minor an on-the-job injury may appear, it is important that it be reported immediately.  Failure to report “on-the-job” injuries immediately may jeopardize any claim for benefits. 

 

In the event that the employee exceeds time limits on any leave granted pursuant to local, state or federal laws, the employer reserves the right to fill the position in accordance with applicable state, federal and local law. 

 

 

Leaving the Company

 

1.  Separation / Termination

 

Employment with the Company is entered into voluntarily.  The employee is free to resign at any time.  Similarly, the Company may terminate the employment relationship at any time it believes it is in its best interest to do so.

 

If the employee resigns from the Company, he/she should submit his/her intention to do so in writing to his/her immediate Supervisor or any member of upper management.  The employee’s letter should contain the date on which his/her resignation is to take effect.  Employees are expected to give two weeks notice to the Company. 

 

On or before the employee’s final day of work, the following administrative matters should be settled: 

           

-          return of all Company property, including but not limited to, employee manuals, employee tools, cell phones or other communication devices, or other equipment or confidential information provided to the

-          employee during the course of his/her employment;

 

-          receipt of information on continuation of medical plans as well as Profit Sharing Plan and Pension Plan information;

 

-          address where last paycheck is to be mailed.  Vacation allowance and personal days will be prorated, adjusted according to number of days taken, and the resulting amount added or deducted from last paycheck.

 

The right to take time off for vacation or any other absence is forfeited upon resignation regardless of reason.  Any absences taken from the time

resignation is tendered to the last day of employment will not be paid.  The

Employee’s last paycheck will include compensation for any remaining pro-rated personal days and vacation days upon resignation or termination.
