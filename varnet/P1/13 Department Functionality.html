Department Functionality

 

Engineering Services

 

Structure:

Our Engineering department is managed by the Director of Engineering Services (DEGS), aka team leader.  The team leader is fully responsible for the functionality of his or her department. 

 

Service Details & Responsibilities:

Under the supervision of the DEGS, this department shall perform the following tasks:

 

Engineering Surveys

Engineering Design

CAD

Property Condition Surveys

Project Management and Budgeting

Specialty Surveys

Code Compliance Studies

Internal Quality Assurance Checks

General Engineering Consulting

 

Prior to the submittal of any bid, an estimate sheet must be generated and approved by the team leader.  Based on the estimate sheet, a proposal can be generated.  Both estimate sheets and standard RFP formats are available through the Ops Log.  During the estimating and proposal process, employee hours are entered against that department’s RFP job number.     

 

Once a proposal is generated, it is presented to upper management for approval along with it’s associated estimate sheet. 

 

Rates:

The most recent applicable rates came into effect on July 1st, 2001.  Refer to Exhibit N for rate table.  Contract Rates are to be used for contracts grater than $4,500.

 

Billing:

Although designated members of the team can issue invoiced for our services, the team leader is ultimately responsible for timely and accurate billing.  Invoices from this department flow from team member to team leader and lastly to the President for final approval.  The President will forward approved invoices to Accounting.  All invoices are generated and adjusted through the Ops Log.

 

Budgeting & Cost Drivers:

The following expenses have been identified as representing the bulk of the overall expenses associated with the operation of Engineering Services.  The department leaded is required to track these expenses on a quarterly basis and create yearly budgets based on historical and projected expectations.  Budgets are due by September 1st of every year and project out for the following year

 

Estimating and RFP’s

On a yearly basis, prior to September 1st, the team leader is to evaluate prior cost drivers and future work expectations and develop a comprehensive budget for each cost driver.  Refer to Exhibit P for a sample budget.  A blank budget report form is available on the following directory XXXXXXXXXXXXXXXXX.

 

Quality Control:

Each team member will implement his or her own quality assurance check as deemed necessary or as directed.  The team leader is ultimately responsible for the quality and accuracy of all work performed by the department.

 

Information Sharing & Flagging:

Work already performed by other departments may benefit Engineering Services.  Similarly, work performed by Engineering Services may be useful to and/or expedite other work performed by other departments.  For these reason, members of Engineering Services must obtain, share and flag information from or relevant to other departments.  For example, if Engineering Services were awarded tenant design work at a particular building, prior to generating and estimate sheet, Engineering ought to determine whether services such as ERI or R&B are performed for the tenant or whether a PGI and/or PLP are available for the building.  Information on other available services may give Engineering a bidding advantage and a cleared picture of the work intricacies.

 

Engineering should be aware of the following services performed by others and should determine whether those services are available and applicable:

PGI – Used to obtain a picture of the electrical distribution system including calculated power availability and sub metered points

R&B – Used to determine usage on building or tenant sub metered loads.  Lease language may also be available.

ERI – Used to obtain information on building PLP, HVAC, tenant lease language, tenant’s estimated usage, tenant’s occupancy, etc.

IRI – Used to determine thermal conditions, if any.

Power Quality – Used to determine whether a power analysis has been performed.

 

Prior to estimating any RFP, members of Engineering Services are required to search the Ops Log by building and/or client to determine whether any other departments have already performed any relevant services.  Should Engineering Services be awarded work, other related departments ought to be informed for purpose of reconciling records.  

 

* In some cases, Engineering estimates may have to include update work to be performed by other departments, such as a PGI update.  

 

Employee Evaluation:

Each team leader is required to submit and employee evaluation sheet semi annually.  The deadlines are January 1st and July 1st.  Refer to Exhibit Q for a sample employee evaluation sheet. 

 

Neatness and Order:

All members of Engineering Services are required to maintain a neat and orderly appearance and work station.
