Electric Rent Inclusion (ERI) Manager

 

 

Overview

Electric rent inclusion management is the process by which electric inclusion tenants are tracked and escalated. 

 

Tenants in commercial buildings generally pay for the use of electricity either through a sub meter, or through electric inclusion.  The manner in which tenant pays is always established in the tenant’s lease document  In the case of electric inclusion, the tenant pays a recurring monthly fee for rent.  This rent  fee generally incorporates an electric charge.  If a rent fee is $1,000, for example, perhaps $800 is for rent and $200 is the electrical component.  In this example, the $200 fee is negotiated between landlord and tenant prior to the lease acceptance.  Because the tenant’s load at the lease negotiation stage is unknown, this fee is derived from certain assumptions.  The landlord however, generally reserves the right to re evaluate the fee after the tenant has taken occupancy to determine whether the fee fairly reflects the actual tenant’s usage.  The negotiated fee can therefore be revised should the landlord, through its consultant, determine that the tenant’s electrical usage equates to an amount higher than the negotiated fee.  The landlord’s consultant uses a survey process to quantify the electrical charges.  Based on  the lighting, general purpose power and HVAC loads present is the tenant’s space, and based on hours of operation for that equipment, the tenant’s KWHR and a KW is estimated.  Once estimated, this data is applied to the rate defined in the lease.  If the charge results in an amount higher that the negotiated fee, the landlord, following lease provisions, has the right to escalate the tenant’s charge.  The new charge would recur monthly and be in affect until such time as another survey is performed showing a drop in tenant’s use of electricity.  The frequency of surveys is also defined in the lease and is generally no more than once per year.  The landlord may elect not to survey a tenant at all. 

 

The survey process is not  the only method used to escalate electric inclusion charges.  Charges are also escalated whenever there is a change in the landlord’s electric rate.  If the landlord’s cost for electricity increase or decrease, the tenant’s charge will also increase or decrease based on the landlord’s percentage change.  For example, if the landlord’s rate for electricity increases by 10% as a result of a rate change mandated by the authorities, the landlord has the right, following lease provisions, to pass on a 10% increase to all its electric inclusion tenants.  This increase would recur monthly until another rate change occurs or until a survey (described above) is performed.      

 

Tenant inclusion charges are also subject to one time fees.  The utility generally incorporates an adjustment charge on every bill.  It can be said that the adjustment charge reflects constant fluctuations in the cost of living and volatile energy prices.  On a quarterly basis, these adjustment charges are evaluated to determine whether the landlord’s cost for the quarter increased or decreased with respect to baseline.  The baseline is established either by the lease, or by rate changes, whichever came last.  These one time fees (or credits) are passed on to the tenants as adjustment charges, commonly termed MAC charges.

 

Genergy engages in ERI services to monitor escalations and ensure that the landlord recovers expenses on a one time basis (in case of MAC adjustments) or on a recurring basis (in cases of surveys or rate changes). 

 

ERI Manager is a web based application developed by Genergy for the purpose of monitoring and maintaining electric rent inclusion records and escalations.  Utility Manager (discussed in section XX), on the other hand, is a web based application aimed at monitoring and maintaining sub metered tenants and charges. 

 

Lease Abstracts

A lease abstract contains information on tenant activity and landlord’s electric recovery method.  In many cases, Genergy receives lease abstracts from the client so that our records are maintained up to date.  A lease abstract is important because it is the first and most affective method of notification.  An abstract will contain information on tenants moving in, moving out, new tenants signing a lease, new license agreements, existing tenants expanding space, or existing tenants giving back space.  Most important though, in each case the abstract will contain specifics on dates, square footages, tenant numbers, and the method to be used for recapturing electricity costs, ie; rent inclusion or sub metering. 

All abstracts are received by Energy Services.  Depending on the nature of the abstract, Energy Services will disseminate information accordingly.  Below is a guideline for the proper dissemination of information:

 

In the event of a move in notice:

Energy Services (ES) will update ERI manager if the tenant is on electric inclusion.  If the tenant is sub metered, ES will inform the appropriate account manager who will in turn expect a rise on the sub meter usage.  

 

In the event of a move out notice:

ES will update ERI manager if the tenant is on electric inclusion.  If the tenant is sub metered, ES will inform the appropriate account manager who will in turn expect a drop on the sub meter usage.  The account manager may also be asked to submit a final sub meter bill. 

 

In the event of a new lease scenario:

ES will update ERI manager if the tenant is on electric inclusion.  If the tenant is sub metered, ES will inform the appropriate account manager who will in turn update Utility Manger.  Regardless of landlord’s methods for recapturing electric costs, ES will notify Tech services who will in turn expect to receive a drawing for review.  In the case of sub metered tenants, Tech Services will also coordinate metering work.   

 

In the event of a new license agreement:

ES will update ERI manager if the tenant is on electric inclusion.  If the tenant is sub metered, ES will inform the appropriate account manager who will in turn update Utility Manger.  Regardless of landlord’s methods for recapturing electric costs, ES will notify Tech services who will in turn expect to receive a drawing for review.  In the case of sub metered tenants, Tech Services will also coordinate metering work.   

 

In the event of an existing tenant expanding space:

ES will update ERI manager if the tenant is on electric inclusion.  If the tenant is sub metered, ES will inform the appropriate account manager who will in turn update Utility Manger and expect increased usage.  Regardless of landlord’s methods for recapturing electric costs, ES will notify Tech services who will in turn expect to receive a drawing for review.  In the case of sub metered tenants, Tech Services will also coordinate metering work.

 

In the event of an existing tenant giving up space:

ES will update ERI manager if the tenant is on electric inclusion.  If the tenant is sub metered, ES will inform the appropriate account manager who will in turn update Utility Manger and expect lower usage.  Regardless of landlord’s methods for recapturing electric costs, ES will notify Tech services who will in turn expect to receive a drawing for review.  In the case of sub metered tenants, Tech Services will also coordinate metering work.

 

All abstracts received are saved either in their electronic format or in their hard copy format.  In addition, ES will print and save every abstract in the Lease Abstracts binder in date order.
