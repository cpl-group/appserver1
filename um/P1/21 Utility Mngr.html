Utility Manager

 

Overview

Utility Manager is the application through which sub metered tenants are tracked and billed. 

 

Tenants in commercial buildings generally pay for the use of electricity either through a sub meter, or through electric inclusion.  The manner in which the tenant pays is always established in the tenant’s lease document.  In the case of sub meter, the tenant pays a monthly bill calculated based on the tenant’s usage as registered by a sub meter or sub meters, tenant’s applicable rate as determined by the lease, and the bill period covered established by the landlord’s utility bill cycle.  

 

In 1996, Genergy created an application called Utility Manager (UM) to monitor and maintain information on sub metered tenants along with their corresponding sub meters, usage history and invoicing.  Since 1996, UM  has become a complex web enabled database It contains information on lease set up parameters such as tenant name, tenant number, address, square footage occupied, applicable rates, assigned meters, tax exempt status, and billing conditions such as coincidental demand and administrative fee. 

 

On behalf of the landlord, Genergy causes the meters to be read at the end of every bill cycle, which varies from building to building, and causes the readings to be entered in the UM application.   Once entered, the readings serve as a basis for calculating the tenant’s electricity bill.
