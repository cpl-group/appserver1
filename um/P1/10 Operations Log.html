Operations Log

 

 

Overview

The Operations Log (Ops Log) is currently designed to issue and tracks activity on reference numbers, aka job numbers, or proposal numbers, aka RFP’s.  Every proposal, every project, even internal or development projects, have an assigned tracking number.  This number is used to identify proposal, projects and internal work for various reasons and to identify projects from a cost and billing standpoint.  In the near future, the Ops Log will be enhanced and fully integrated into Genergy’s intranet.  Once enhances, the Ops Log will be renamed to ‘The Main Module’ 

 

The Ops Log interface is accessed through Genergy’s intranet.  Reference number can be found by filtering various parameters, time sheets can be accessed, billing can be generated, correspondence, proposals and purchase orders can be issued, jobs can be estimated, existing electronic files can be viewed, or a new RFP’s or job number can be created along with new client information.    

           

Standard RFP Formats

Each RFP (Request For Proposal) will be assigned a tracking number via the Ops Log interface.  From the Ops Log, the user can search for an RFP, update status to job, preview existing RFP’s, create new RFP’s, preview hours posted to RFP’s, and so on.  Upon exiting the Log, you will be prompted to save any changes made to ‘RFP status’.

Reference Numbers; aka Job Numbers

Once a proposal is accepted by the client, it becomes a job. The tracking number assigned to the RFP automatically becomes the job number.  All projects performed by Genergy have a job number, there are no exceptions.  These electronic job numbers are nothing more than electronic folders stored on our network server ELISA.  Each folder contains files, or documents pertinent to the project.  These files include word documents, CAD drawings, specifications, excel spreadsheets, power point presentations, faxes, proposals, POs, correspondence and so on.  The Ops Log provides is an interface screen that gives the user easy access to all electronic job numbers.  From the Ops Log, the user can search for a record, update job status, preview existing electronic files, create new electronic documents, preview hours posted to the job, generate billing and so on.  Upon exiting the Log, you will be prompted to save any changes made to ‘job status’ parameters.  However, electronic documents such as proposals, letters, drawings, faxes etc. are crated, saved, or altered at the user’s discretion.

 

 * No RFP’s or job documents already issued to clients should ever be deleted or overwritten. 

 

 

Saving Your Data 

Generally, and as already mentioned above, the first document issued to a client is a proposal, or RFP.  Once accepted, the proposal becomes a job.  Throughout the course of the work, other electronic documents, reports, drawings and so on are generated and issued to the client as part of Genergy’s scope.  Every document issued, whether RFP or other, must be identified with the tracking number plus a two decimal extension.  For example, if a proposal were the first document issued under RFP number 1234, the proposal would be identified as ‘1234.00’.  A description may be added to the ID number extension for easier identification.  In the case above, the proposal could be identified as ‘1234.00 proposal’.  If the proposal were excepted by the client, for instance, and a CAD drawing were then generated as part of our service, the drawing would be saved as ‘1234.01 lobby drawing’.  Other electronic document follow chronological extension numbering.  No files should exist with a description only.  All files must have a numeric reference number and progressive extension number.  The extension numbers are crucial to establishing chronological sequence of events. 

 

Correspondence

Correspondence includes status updates, faxes, memos, notices, letters, final reports and any other information not related to proposals, drawings or specifications.  New correspondence can be generated through the Ops Log and automatically saved under the reference number.  You will be prompted to enter the file name.  Proper extensions numbers are a must.     

 

Estimates

Estimates are used to identify projects costs and must be generated prior to issuing a proposal.  Through the Ops Log, existing estimates can be previewed, new estimates can be generated, and change orders can be issues.  Estimates are essential to determine and propose our fee to render the service.  The estimates also serve as a baseline for project performance.  Various estimate formats are accessible through the Ops Log.   New estimates require a file name.  During the estimating and proposal process, employee hours are entered against that department’s RFP job number.           

 

Change Orders

A Change Order signifies a change to an existing scope and its associated fee.  Existing estimates and related proposals are changed only when the existing scope of work has expanded or contracted per the client’s request.  A Change Order is not to be confused with a new estimate.  

 

New estimates should only be generated when a proposed scope of work is an not related and not a continuation of an existing assignment. 

 

Purchase Orders

Purchase Orders issued by Genergy are generated through the intranet.  A purchase order is issued to a vendor as a formal authorization for the purchase materials, equipment or services (sub contracting).  The purchase order provides standard information on an order placed including vendor name, shipping date and location, order number, and an itemized listing of materials, equipment or services sought along with corresponding cost allocations for each.  Costs for materials, equipment or services should match the estimated materials, equipment or services.  The following procedures ought to be followed:

 

·          A purchase order is created by the individual who is requisitioning the product or service.

·          The purchase order is approved by the team leader and presented to the COO.  The COO may question nature of expenditure at that time.

·          When the service is complete or material is received an invoice, is issued by the vendor. 

·          That invoice is approved by the employee who requested the materials or services.

·          Invoice is matched by accounting to purchase order. 

·          Check is issued if the invoice and purchase order are with in tolerance.

·          Check is signed by the President who may at this time questions any expenditure. Back up is then presented by accounting.  The President does not typically approve purchase orders.

 

Purchase orders for office and/or departmental supplies less than $100.00 may be approved by department directors.  Purchase orders for supplies above $100 require department approval and upper management approval.

 

Purchase orders under $4,000.00 for the re-sale of supplies, equipment or services may be approved by the department directors.  For example, sub contracting, metering equipment, equipment rentals, sub consulting, or other goods or services necessary to execute accepted RFP’s may be ordered without upper management approval by the department directors provided that department directors have received a client purchase order authorizing the proposed work to proceed and the expense is not grater than the expense already itemized through the estimating process.

 

Time Sheets and Time Allocation

Although each employee through a different user interface called ‘Time Sheet’ allocates time to jobs, a summary of time posted to a specific job is viewable through the intranet.  The summary screen will not only display time posted by date and employees, but also provide an option to invoice the job for services rendered.  We call this procedure ‘Billing’.      

 

Billing

Each project manager is responsible for timely and accurate billing.  Invoices are auto generated through the intranet.  Prior to generating an invoice, the user must verify that the client selected is in fact the party to be billed.  The client selection will appear on the invoice heading once the invoice is generated.  The client selected will be the invoice recipient.  During the estimating and proposal process, employee hours are entered against that department’s RFP job number.  Time spent on RFP’s is generally not billable.         

 

The invoice module provides an area for typing the invoice description.  The default description always begins with ‘This is an invoice for services rendered in connection with…’.  The user should commence typing having considered this default opening statement.  The description must be as specific as possible, must include location of services, Genergy’s job number, and must reference any prior documents, approved proposals, client PO’s etc.  Although every invoice is unique, Exhibit L may be used as a reference to illustrate various invoice formats. 

 

The most common cause of delayed payment manifests itself because an invoice is either not received, addressed to the wrong party, or lacking proper description.  The client should never hold payment for any of these reasons.
