Time Sheets

 

At the end of every workday, time sheet information for each employee is auto accessed and management reports are generated.  Employees are therefore required to ‘fill out’ time sheets on a daily basis, prior to 6 PM. 

 

Entering time sheet information is a delicate an extremely important responsibility.  Aside from providing valuable information on job cost, time allotment per job is necessary to determine billable hours and to provide a record sheet on work performed.  Time posted to RFP numbers will be treated as overhead.  Time posted to jobs will be considered billable.

 

Time allotted to an RFP or a job may be as little as five minutes, for a fax or a phone call.  Although it is difficult to remember every conversation and every single task, employees are asked to do so.  Maintaining a time sheet should be part of the everyday recurring routine.  Every task assigned has a reference number, either RFP or job, there are no exceptions.  Therefore employees must know their task and properly allot time to the corresponding RFP number or job number. 

 

All time sheet entries are reference number driven.  The reference number entered causes the display to default the building and description entered via the Ops Log interface screen.  The user must provide detailed information in addition to the default information.  The information entered by the user should include visits to the site, phone conversations, faxes, submittals, and should reference other work or other employees with whom work was performed.  Time sheet information is reviewed constantly and may be disapproved.  For more on proper time sheet entries see Exhibit M.
