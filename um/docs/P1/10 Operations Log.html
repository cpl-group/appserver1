Operations Log

 

Overview

The Operations Log (Ops Log) is currently designed to issue and tracks activity on reference numbers, aka job numbers, or proposal numbers, aka RFP’s.  Every proposal, every project, even internal or development projects, have an assigned RFP tracking number or a job number.  This number is used to identify proposal/projects for various reasons and to identify projects from a cost and billing standpoint  

 

Time sheets and Purchase Orders (PO) are entered through the Ops Log thereby providing information and labor and equipment costs.  Invoicing is also performed through the Ops Log thereby providing information on revenue.  The difference between cost and revenue is Genergy’s heartbeat.  In addition, the Log tracks electronic files by allowing the user to access a user interface representing a specified reference numbers. 

 

Through the Log interface, a reference number can be found by filtering various parameters, time sheets can be accessed, billing can be generated, correspondence, proposals and purchase orders can be issued, jobs can be estimated, existing electronic files can be viewed, or a new RFP’s or job number can be created along with new client information.

Reference Numbers; aka Job Numbers

All projects performed by Genergy have an electronic reference number, or job number.  These electronic job numbers are nothing more than electronic folders stored on our network server ELISA.  Each folder contains files, or documents pertinent to the project.  These files include word documents, CAD drawings, specifications, excel spreadsheets, power point presentations, faxes, proposals, POs, correspondence and so on.  The Ops Log provides is an interface screen that gives the user easy access to all electronic job numbers.  From the Ops Log, the user can search for a record, update job status, preview existing electronic files, create new electronic documents, preview hours posted to the job, generate billing and so on.  Upon exiting the Log, you will be prompted to save any changes made to ‘job status’ parameters.  However, electronic documents such as proposals, letters, drawings, faxes etc. are crated, saved, or altered at the user’s discretion.

 

 * No RFP’s or project documents already issued to clients should ever be deleted or overwritten. 

 

Saving Your Data 

Generally, the first document issued to a client is a proposal.  Once the proposal is accepted, the proposal status changes to active and throughout the course of the work, other electronic documents, reports, drawings and so on are generated and issued to the client as part of Genergy’s scope.  Every document issued, whether RFP or other, must be identified with the corresponding tracking/job number plus a two decimal extension.  For example, if a proposal were issued under RFP number 1234, the proposal would be identified as ‘1234.00’.  A description may be added to the ID number extension for easier identification.  In the case above, the proposal could be identified as ‘1234.00 proposal’.  If the proposal were excepted by the client, for instance, and a CAD drawing were generated as part of our service, the drawing would be saved as ‘1234.01 lobby drawing’.  Other electronic document follow chronological numbering.  No files should exist with a description only.  All files must have a numeric reference number and progressive extension number.  The extension numbers are crucial to establishing chronological sequence of events. 

 

 

Correspondence

Correspondence includes status updates, faxes, memos, notices, letters, final reports and any other information not related to proposals, drawings or specifications.  New correspondence can be generated through the Ops Log and automatically saved under the reference number.  You will be prompted to enter the file name.     

 

Estimates

Estimates are used to identify projects costs and must be generated prior to issuing a proposal.  Through the Ops Log, existing estimates can be previewed, new estimates can be generated, and change orders can be issues.  Estimates are essential to determine and propose our fee to render the service.  The estimates also serve as a baseline for project performance.  Various estimate formats are accessible through the Ops Log.   New estimates require a file name.  During the estimating and proposal process, employee hours are entered against that department’s RFP job number.            

 

Change Orders

A Change Order signifies a change to an existing scope and its associated fee.  Existing estimates and related proposals are changed only when the existing scope of work has expanded or contracted per the client’s request.  A Change Order is not to be confused with a new estimate.  

 

New estimates should only be generated when a proposed scope of work is an not related and not a continuation of an existing assignment. 

 

Purchase Orders

Purchase Orders issued by Genergy are generated through the Ops Log.  A purchase order is issued to a vendor as a formal authorization for the purchase materials, equipment or services (sub contracting).  The purchase order provides standard information on an order placed including vendor name, shipping date and location, order number, and an itemized listing of materials, equipment or services sought along with corresponding cost allocations for each.  Costs for materials, equipment or services should match the estimated materials, equipment or services.  The following procedures ought to be followed:

 

·          A purchase order is created by the individual who is requisitioning the product or service.

·          The purchase order is approved by the team leader and presented to the COO.  The COO may question nature of expenditure at that time.

·          When the service is complete or material is received an invoice, is issued by the vendor. 

·          That invoice is approved by the employee who requested the materials or services.

·          Invoice is matched by accounting to purchase order. 

·          Check is issued if the invoice and purchase order are with in tolerance.

·          Check is signed by the President who may at this time questions any expenditure. Back up is then presented by accounting.  The President does not typically approve purchase orders.

 

Time Sheets and Time Allocation

Although each employee through a different user interface called ‘Time Sheet’ allocates time to jobs, a summary of time posted to a specific job is viewable through the Ops Log interface.  The summary screen will not only display time posted by date and employees, but also provide an option to invoice the job for services rendered.  We call this procedure ‘Billing’.      

 

Billing

Each project manager is responsible for timely and accurate billing.  Invoices are auto generated through the Ops Log.  Prior to generating an invoice, the user must verify that the client selected is in fact the party to be billed.  The client selection will appear on the invoice heading once the invoice is generated.  The client selected will be the invoice recipient.  During the estimating and proposal process, employee hours are entered against that department’s RFP job number.  Time spent on RFP’s is generally not billable. .         

 

The Log provides an area for typing the invoice description.  The default description (not viewable) always begins with ‘This is an invoice for services rendered in connection with…’.  The user should commence typing having considered this default opening statement.  The description must be as specific as possible, must include location of services, Genergy’s job number, and must reference any prior documents, approved proposals, client PO’s etc.  Although every invoice is unique, Exhibit L may be used as a reference to illustrate various invoice formats. 

 

The most common cause of delayed payment manifests itself because an invoice is either not received, addressed to the wrong party, or lacking proper description.  The client should never hold payment for any of these reasons.
