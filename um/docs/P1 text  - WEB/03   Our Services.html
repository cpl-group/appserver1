Our Services

Overview

Genergy’s provides a broad range of services to Real Estate property owners and managers.  Currently we provide our services to approximately 45 buildings covering approximately 35,000,000 square feet.  Our services include:

PGI (Power Grid Identification)

A color coded schematic detailing the electrical distribution and metering layout of a given building or facility, from entrance point to primary distribution.  The highlights of this service include one line diagrams differentiating between power serving tenants and power serving building systems, power availability charts showing design capacity, and metering schedules.  The PGI can also be modified to contrast design capacity vs. actual power used thereby yielding spare available power.  Genergy can make all of the PGI information available via the world wide web.  The PGI is updated upon request or automatically via Drawing Review services.

Drawing Review Services

Genergy reviews tenant alteration drawings on behalf of the Landlord, our client.  A tenant generally issues alteration drawings to the Landlord as a formal request to alter leased space.  Genergy received these drawings from the Landlord and is asked to review and comment.  As part our work, lease language is reviewed along with the tenant’s proposed alteration.  Comments are issued and the tenant’s progression of work is monitored.  Upon work completion, site surveys are performed to compare tenant drawing submittal vs. actual field work performed.  Variances are reconciled and the PGI record and power availability is updated. 

Branch Circuit Tracing

Electrical branch circuitry in commercial buildings is ever changing.  Over time, circuits are added, deleted and detailed records are often lost.  The circuit origin of many outlets and lighting fixtures are therefore often unknown.  Branch circuit tracing is performed when an owner or a tenant wishes to identify circuit distribution.  This is particularly important in computer room applications to avoid overloading of circuits and resulting equipment shut downs.  Branch circuit tracing and identification is also crucial for metering applications.

Metering Services

Genergy sells, installs, warrants, maintains and monitors, electrical metering devices and systems.  Our newest product line includes revenue grade power quality meters connected to a remotely accessed data collection system.  The system acquires, stores and processes, among many things, interval or ‘time/date’ stamped data.  This data is crucial in today’s energy procurement and pricing.

Reading & Billing

Landlords buy electricity in bulk for a building or facility and redistribute that electricity to building tenants.  As part of leasing agreements, these tenants are required to refund the Landlord for electricity use.  There are two primary methods used by the Landlord to calculate and collect fees for electric use.  The first method involves and electric inclusion charge (ERI), which is a flat monthly charge regardless of actual usage.  The second method involves sub metering, or the installation of devices for the purpose of monitoring actual electric use.  The concept in metering applications is that a tenant will only pay for actual energy used.  The kilowatt and/or Kilowatt Hour use recorded by the sub meters is used to calculate the tenant’s electrical bill.  Often Genergy is retained to read sub meter usage and bill tenants on the Landlords behalf.  Although installing and reading data collections systems is a technical challenge, billing electrical usage is far more complex.  It involves accuracy and scrutiny and an understanding of usage patterns, rates, and billing methods.  Genergy implements state of the art equipment and software to monitor, read and bill electrical usage. 

Electric Rent Inclusion (ERI) Management Services

Tenants who agree to pay the Landlord on an inclusion basis do not require a meter.  Inclusion tenants pay  a flat monthly fee for electric use.  This electric fee however is subject to landlord escalations.  ERI escalation are calculated by Genergy on behalf of the Landlord whenever the Landlord’s cost for electricity changes or whenever the tenant’s load increases.  Genergy tracks increases by entering building costs and tenant charges into our database and by monitoring market activity.  Surveys of tenant spaces are also performed from time to time to determine whether tenant’s use of power has increased since the lease commencement data.  Energy issues letters to tenants whenever an increase to their electric inclusion fees is calculated.

PLP (Public Lighting and Power ) Surveys

Virtually all buildings and facilities use electricity to power base systems like HVAC, elevators, public corridors, lobbies or receptions, rest rooms, exhaust fans and so on.  Because often Landlords are entitled, according to lease language, to pass on operating costs, PLP surveys are performed to establish electric use and related electric expense.  The Landlord will divide the building’s PLP expense proportionally among building tenants if leasing agreements allow it.    

Revenue vs. Expense Profiling

In many cases Genergy provides Reading and Billing services coupled with ERI services.  The integration of the two gives us a clear picture of electric expense vs. monies recaptured via sub metering and electric inclusion.  These profiles compare expense vs. revenue per building on a month by month basis.  Sites can be combined to show aggregate expense vs. revenue.

Overtime HVAC Surveys 

Landlords generally provide cooling, heating and/or ventilation during standard working hours.  If tenants elect to work off hours and require cooling, heating or ventilation, the Landlord is often entitled to charge a fee for providing the service.  Genergy performs surveys of building mechanical systems to estimate the hourly cost to provide such services.     

    

IRI (Infrared Radiometer Inspection)

Excessive heat may indicate equipment malfunction.  Infrared or thermal imaging is periodically performed on electrical distribution and mechanical components as a form of preventive maintenance.  Reports are issued based on various surveyed conditions including temperature.  The highlights of this service include the ability to spot areas of energy waste and the ability to monitor improvement or degradation of equipment over time.

Power Quality

Our firm owns multiple power analyzers.  These machines ‘connect’ to electrical circuitry and plot characteristics such as volts, amps, Kilowatts, Kilowatt Hours, Power Factor, and percentage of harmonic distortion.  Power analyzers are often used to troubleshoot faults such as overloads, to monitor equipment operation and verify energy use.

MEP Engineering

Genergy provides a wide range of engineering services that include code compliance studies, general consulting, feasibility studies, design, construction management, and property assessment and condition surveys.
