General Office

Hours of Operation

The office is open Monday through Friday, except legal holidays (see Company Details, Section D), between the hours of 7 AM and 6 PM.   Business hours are from 8 AM to 4:30 PM.  Employees work 8 hour days within either of the above time frames.  Although the workday may begin at different times for different employees, usually depending on the employees commute, it is always pre defined on an employee by employee basis before the first day of work.  Lunch duration is ½ hour. 

Reception

The main telephone switchboard, among other things, resides at the reception desk (aka: front desk).  not only do all phone calls come in and out through the front desk, but so do all employees, our clients, all deliveries, all messengers and the mail.  It can be said that the reception is much like Genergy’s nerve center.  The receptionist must not only greet callers, but provide guidance whenever callers need immediate assistance, in case of emergencies, or whenever callers have general questions.  The receptionist therefore should always be polite, aware of each employee’s location, responsive to emergencies and knowledgeable enough to answer general inquiries.  The receptionist should also greet incomers, whether employees or clients, this is essential in promoting a positive environment.

Punching In and Out

Each employee is issued a door access card.  Employees are required to ‘punch in’ in the morning upon entering and ‘punch out’ in the evening when leaving.  Access card information may be used to verify timeliness.   Employees are also required to punch in and out when returning or leaving for appointments.  In addition to punching in and out, employees must explicitly inform the receptionist of their departure and return.   

Office Absence

Employee are required to inform the receptionist directly of lateness or absence, regardless of whether the absence is due to personal or vacation time. 

Lateness

Repeated tardiness is not acceptable.  There will be a ½ hour grace period per week allotted to each employee.  Thereafter employees are subject to disciplinary action.    The Chief Financial Officer (CFO) will determine when and how to discipline repeated lateness.  Generally, the CFO elects to utilize the employee’s personal time against lateness.  When personal time is not available, pay deductions are implemented.  Each instance will be addressed by the CFO.  It is not company policy to allow employees an earlier start or a later finish to compensate for lateness. 

Expenses

Employees are to fill out expense reports on a weekly basis.  All reports must include valid receipts and a job number for each expense.  Expense report are submitted to the department leader for approval.  After approval, department leader are to submit expense reports to the Chief Operating Officer (COO) for final approval.  The Accounting will thereafter process payment.    

Voice Mail and E Mail

Each employee is assigned a specific telephone extension with voice mail and a dedicated e mail address.  Both phone and e mail are for office use only and may be monitored.  Your voice mail should be set up using a professional tone and should include your cellular number in case of emergency.  Whenever leaving for vacation, your voice mail should be changed to indicate your absence and your return.  Use of e mail for personal reasons should be minimal or none at all.  Through e mail, our office risks virus infection.  Minimizing e mail traffic is a primary IT concern. 

Internet Access

Through the Internet, our office further risks virus infection.  Internet access is reserved for office use only and may be monitored.

Network and Electronic File Access (security levels)

All correspondence, all proposal, drawings, specifications, schematics, layouts, studies, reports, photos, time sheets, and every other aspect of Genergy’s files and operational info. is saved on our Virtual Private Network.  All office computers are connected to Genergy’s network.  No work should be saved on local hard drives.  All work is to be performed via network resources.  Each employee is assigned a password and login combination.  This customized access combination grants or denies access to various network files.  In case you are erroneously denied access to a specific file, contact the Director of Network Operations (DNO) to request access.  Refer to Exhibit B for or network tree structure.  All employees are expected to know and understand the network structure and the placement of files.    

Security

The building superintendent generally opens, closes and secures the office during normal working days.  During off hours, keys are required for the lobby door and for elevator access.  Our office is equipped with an alarm station and motion sensors that become active during off hours.  Unannounced visits to the office during off hours are not welcome.  The Office is officially closed at 6 PM.  Employees working past 6 PM must be obtain permission from their supervisor and must arrange to close and secure the office.       

ID Cards

Identification cards are issued to all employees.  These cards should be visible during working hours especially while out of the office.

Supplies

The office support staff will distribute and order supplies on a regular basis or upon request.  Because supplies represent a large portion of the office expense, ordering office supplies requires approval.  Orders should be in bulk.  Requisition forms, Exhibit C, must be approved by the department leader and submitted to the office support staff.

Copy, Scan, Print, Paper, and Recycling Stations

These stations are available throughout the office.  For the benefit of all, employees are asked to keep these areas clutter free.

Your Work Area

With the exception of private offices, all work areas are open and visible.  Cluttered desks suggest lack of organization.  Each employee will be provided with ample accessories to organize their files and supplies.  Employees are expected to maintain a neat and organized work area.  No one is permitted to tape anything to the walls.  Cork boards will be provided upon request.  Only work related pictures and diagrams may be hung on cork boards.  Employees are expected to clean work areas each evening prior to leaving the office.

Company and Department Meetings

The entire office staff is scheduled to meet informally with the Chief Executive Officer (CEO), President, and/or Chief Operating Officer (COO) on the first Wednesday of every month at 9 AM.  During this meeting, employees are encouraged to speak out on any work related or non work related subjects.  Department leaders are scheduled to meet with the President and COO every other Friday at 9 AM.  Department leaders are scheduled to meet with their respective staff on a weekly basis or as required.   

Recycling

Energy awareness has never been as widespread as it is today.   Like many other companies in the energy industry, Genergy encourage recycling.  Employees are asked to participate.

Filing Procedures

Every assignment has an associated  reference number.  All reference numbers have both electronic folders and physical folders for hard copies.  The network administrator organizes electronic folders.  Employees access these electronic folders through the Operations Log (discussed later) to view, edit, or generate proposals, correspondences, invoices, drawings etc. .  The physical folder containing hard copies, client purchase order, client correspondence, field notes etc. should always be in the possession of the project manager. Physical folders should be organized in (3) categories, namely ‘RFP’, ‘On Going’, and ‘Billed awaiting invoice copy’ and should be available and accessible to everyone.  When the work is complete and accounting has submitted a copy of our services rendered invoice, the folder should be immediately filed in number sequence.      

The Lounge

All Employees are welcome to utilize the lounge area as long as each does his or her part to keep the lounge clean, that includes removing perishables from the refrigerator.  The lounge may be used as a gathering place, during breaks, at lunch time, and in the morning prior to punching in and the commencement of your work day.  Employees are not permitted to have food on their desks during regular business hours. 

Conference Room

Any employee can schedule the conference room.  To schedule the conference room, select it from you Microsoft Outlook calendar.

Contact List

A comprehensive employee and client contact list is available at the reception desk.

Radios & Phones

Employees may be assigned a radio, a cellular phone, a pager or a combination of devices that facilitate communications.  These devices are for office related use only or for emergencies and may be monitored.

Time Sheets

Employees are paid either on a weekly basis or an hourly basis.  Regardless, labor hours are Genergy’s single biggest expense.  Labor hours are also Genergy’s single biggest source of revenue.  Needless to say, all hours on billable projects must be accounted for to secure the company’s future.  Time posted to jobs is therefore constantly reviewed.  Employees are required to fill out time sheets on a daily basis and to submit those time sheets on time on a weekly basis.  Employees who submit time sheets late may experience payroll delays.   Filling out time sheets is one of the most important tasks performed by an employee.

New Employee

Welcome!!!!  Be sure to read this manual, contact the accounting department to finalize your paperwork, contact IT for access to Ops Log and Outlook calendar, inquire about your ID card, you door access card, familiarize yourself with our services, our clients, our procedures and rules, ask your questions, read, accept and sign the terms and conditions detailed in Section VI, Company Details, use your initiative, be open to constructive criticism, relax, familiarize yourself with the chain of command, and always be honest.
