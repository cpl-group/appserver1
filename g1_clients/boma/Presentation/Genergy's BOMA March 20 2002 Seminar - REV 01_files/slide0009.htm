Examples
•Example: Your Electrical Contractor tells you that to install the requested additional capacity for the tenant in the previous example he is planning to install 4-4/0 AWG conductors in a 3 inch conduit.  Is this correct?
•
• In a 208 Volt distribution scenario, keeping the tenant requirements approximately 200 Amps  and looking at the “Current Carrying Capacity Chart” (NYC Electric Code, Article 5, Table 1) we see that for the most commonly used insulation, THW, the most suited conductor sizes is of either a 3/0 AWG (200 Amps) or a 4/0 AWG (230 Amps).  To keep a certain degree of safety margin it would be advisable to opt for installing a 4/0 AWG riser. • Looking at the “Number of Conductors in Conduit” chart (NYC Electric Code,. Article 4 Table 1) we can see that the minimum allowable conduit size for four (4) 4/0 AWG conductors is 3 inches.
•
• The contractor statement is correct.
