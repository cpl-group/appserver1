Basic Formulas
  • True & Apparent Power Formulas
  • for a 3 phase 4 wire distribution system:


  • True Power = Volts x Amps x 1.732 x PF = Watts
  • Example: Power = 208V x 380Amps x 1.732 x .85 = 116,362 Watts or ≈116.3 KW
  • Apparent Power = Volts x Amps x 1.732 = Volt Amps
  • Example: Power = 208V x 380Amps x 1.732 = 136,897 VA or ≈ 136.8 KVA
7
Basic Formulas
  • To Calculate Watts per Square Foot:


  • True Power ÷ Square Feet = W/Sq.Ft.


  • Example: A prospective tenants engineer has told you that the projected power requirements for the space are of approximately 60KW.  The space in question is about 9,600 square feet.


  • 60,000 Watts ÷ 9600 Square Feet = 6.25 Watts / Sq. Ft.

Examples
  • Example: A tenant is requesting 10W/sq.ft. to be utilized for standard lighting and power circuits for an area of 10,000 Square feet.  How many watts and how many amps those this translate to?


  • 10 W/Sq.Ft. x 10,000 Sq.Ft. = 100,000 Watts or 100KW


  • In a building with a 208 Volt 3 phase 4 wire distribution system:
  • Amps = 100KW ÷ (208V x 1.732 x .85PF) = 326.5 Amps


  • In a building with a 480 Volt 3 phase 4 wire distribution system:
  • Amps = 100KW ÷ (480V x 1.732 x .85PF) = 141.5 Amps
10
Examples
  • Example: A tenant who is occupying 19,500 square feet of space in the building, has a lease clause that allows it to utilize up to 6 Watts per square foot.  The tenants power is currently being supplied by a dedicated 500MCM riser.  The tenant is asking for an additional 200Amps to feed a planned data center.  Is the tenant within its lease power allotment?
  • First, lets calculate how many watts per square foot are being delivered to the tenant:
  • From the Current Carrying Capacity Chart (NYC Electric Code, Article 5, Table 1) we see that for the most commonly used insulation, THW, a 500MCM conductor can carry up to 380 Amps.
  • Since the buildings power distribution system is a 208/120 Volt then:
  • Total Power Delivered = 208 Volts x 380 Amps x 1.732 x .85 = 116.3 KW
  • Watts Per Sq.Ft. delivered = 116,300 ÷ 19,500 = 5.96 W/Sq.Ft.


  •   The tenant is currently utilizing all of the distribution resources allotted as per lease agreement.  Any additional power distribution to this tenant should be charged for at prevailing rates.  As a general guideline these range from $200 to $400 per ampere of additional capacity requested.
11
Examples
  • Example: Your Electrical Contractor tells you that to install the requested additional capacity for the tenant in the previous example he is planning to install 4-4/0 AWG conductors in a 3 inch conduit.  Is this correct?


  • In a 208 Volt distribution scenario, keeping the tenant requirements approximately 200 Amps  and looking at the Current Carrying Capacity Chart (NYC Electric Code, Article 5, Table 1) we see that for the most commonly used insulation, THW, the most suited conductor sizes is of either a 3/0 AWG (200 Amps) or a 4/0 AWG (230 Amps).  To keep a certain degree of safety margin it would be advisable to opt for installing a 4/0 AWG riser.
  • Looking at the Number of Conductors in Conduit chart (NYC Electric Code,. Article 4 Table 1) we can see that the minimum allowable conduit size for four (4) 4/0 AWG conductors is 3 inches.


  • The contractor statement is correct.
